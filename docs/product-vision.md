# Product Vision

## Summary

The Support Hub is the operational core for a company that needs better service management, customer support, knowledge management, and reporting. It should feel modern, calm, and polished rather than like an "AI product", while still using automation and AI where they make support work easier.

The broader platform can become a family of connected hubs:

- **Support Hub** for tickets, incidents, service requests, SLAs, and agent workflows.
- **Knowledge Hub** for articles, self-service, and operational knowledge.
- **Customer Hub** for customer records, account context, and future CRM capability.
- **Glass Hub** for management reporting across all operational systems.

## Why Start With Support Hub

Support is the right starting point because it creates the first shared operational dataset.

Support touches customers, internal teams, service quality, product issues, knowledge gaps, SLA performance, and recurring operational pain. If the company has limited systems today, support becomes the best place to establish clean process, clean data, and clean reporting habits.

Starting with CRM first would be useful, but it risks becoming a contact database before the company understands the real service workflows. Starting with reporting first would create dashboards without trusted operational data underneath. The Support Hub gives the company a live workflow and the data exhaust needed to build meaningful reporting.

## Product Principles

- Simple by default, configurable when needed.
- Every workflow should produce reportable data.
- ITIL should guide the operating model, not dominate the user experience.
- Knowledge should grow from real support demand.
- AI should be embedded quietly into workflows, not presented as the product's identity.
- Management reporting should not require agents to duplicate admin work.

## Experience Direction

The product should borrow the discipline of ServiceNow and the simplicity of Freshdesk. The visual language should feel closer to a polished advisory or modern enterprise operations product than a loud SaaS landing page.

Useful references:

- ServiceNow for ITIL concepts, service catalog thinking, workflow maturity, and operational control.
- Freshdesk for approachable support workflows, clear ticket views, and easy customer experience.
- Confluence for knowledge hierarchy and article-based collaboration, simplified heavily.
- Power BI and Fabric for the long-term analytics and executive reporting direction.

## Near-Term Outcome

The first milestone is not a complete platform. It is a credible Support Hub foundation:

- Customers can find knowledge or ask for help.
- Agents can manage tickets from multiple channels.
- Support leaders can see operational performance.
- Knowledge gaps become visible.
- Data is structured in a way that can feed future reporting and Fabric.
